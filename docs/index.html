MEet our Team

Meet callie

Callie is a senior studying Business, Entrepreneurship, and Organization with a focus on Entrepreneurship and Technology Management. Callie is known to shred mountains on her bike as well as chords on Guitar Hero. Her Spotify rotation almost exclusively consists of Spain and Argentina’s Top 50, which she can most likely be found listening to whilst getting ripped at the Nelson—the Fitness Center, that is. Not to be mistaken for the Nelson Center for Entrepreneurship—did we mention that Callie’s BEO Capstone group won first place in the Rhode Island Business Plan Competition’s Elevator Pitch Contest? When you see her in the Underground, try guessing what Callie is short for—the possibilities include California, Calliope, Callison, Calamari, or Caligula.

Meet ELI

Eli is a senior studying Literary Arts and Psychology. He’s an INFJ, enneagram type 4w3, Taurus, Hufflepuff, his love language is touch—not that it matters, because love is a lie (which is coincidentally an anagram for Eli!)—and he can evolve into a duck in less than five minutes. You know how some people say they’re lactose intolerant even though they’re definitely going to get mozzarella in their Blue Room sandwich after drinking their almond milk latte? Well Eli is REALLY lactose intolerant. As in, if he so much as looks at a cow his insides turn to stone. If you spy him in the Underground (or anywhere for that matter—you might be lucky enough to catch him at the GCB!), make sure to ask him about his home state of Minnesota, Settlers of Catan, The Magicians, poetry, or the importance of wearing a bike helmet.

meet burke

Manager and Underground Dad™, Burke O'Brien. Burke is a senior studying International Relations and Economics. Every night he sets his alarm for 7:30am to do yoga, and every morning when his alarm goes off he promptly shuts it off. At any given moment, he can be found either in the Underground working on his Panda Planner or in his house playing Super Smash Bros on his Nintendo Switch (wow!). His catchphrases include “Hellö?”, “Thanks, I hate it,” and “What’s up lizards.” This semester one of his goals was to stop biting his nails, and with the help of his Panda Planner he succeeded! If you see Burke in the Underground, congratulate him on his self-improvement.

Meet Rachel

Meet Rachel Rood-Ojalvo aka DJ Rood Girl with the Gentle Touch. She’s a first semester senior concentrating in urban studies with a focus on criminal justice Hurricane Katrina. This past winter break, she went on a 10-day silent Vipassana meditation retreat. She also once lived in the Shakespeare & Company bookstore in Paris, and another bookstore in Madrid. Tune into the Gentle Touch on WBRU, Sunday nights from 8-11pm, and hit her with a follow on Spotify! When you’re in the Underground, toss her some music recommendations—if you’re lucky enough, it might end up on the prestigious ~unofficial~ Underground playlist, No Room for Milk.
